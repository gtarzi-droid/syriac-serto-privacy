Privacy Policy — Syriac Serto Keyboard

Effective date: July 2, 2026
App: Syriac Serto (iOS keyboard and companion app)
Developer: George Tarzi
Contact: tarztech@gmail.com

Syriac Serto does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. The keyboard works entirely on your device.

The keyboard does not use “Full Access”

iOS custom keyboards can optionally request Full Access, which is what would allow a keyboard to use the network or share what you type. Syriac Serto does not request Full Access (RequestsOpenAccess = false). Because of this, the keyboard:

  • cannot access the internet or any network;
  • cannot send anything you type to us or to anyone else;
  • cannot read your typing history, contacts, location, photos, or any other personal information.

Everything you type goes only into the app you are typing in — exactly as it would with Apple’s built‑in keyboards.

Data we collect

None. We do not collect analytics, usage statistics, crash logs, device identifiers, advertising identifiers, or any other data. There are no accounts, no sign‑in, and no third‑party advertising or analytics SDKs in the app.

In‑app purchases

The app offers optional “tips” to support development. These are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store; we never see or receive your payment information. Making a purchase is completely optional and unlocks no personal‑data collection.

Data stored on your device

The app stores nothing about you. Text you type is handled by iOS and the app you are typing into; the keyboard itself keeps no record of it.

Children’s privacy

Because the app collects no data at all, it is safe for users of any age, including children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone.

Fonts

The app includes the Noto Sans Syriac Western font (SIL Open Font License 1.1) and Serto Urhoy by Fr. Joseph Tarzi (Meltho font license, for free distribution). These are bundled for on‑device rendering only and involve no data collection.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this same URL with a new effective date.
